Asking Questions (Cultural blog) 11/11/2014

The most important part of asking any question is framing the problem into as short and concise of a description as possible, while also making clear how the desired solution should work.This goes for questions posed to people and to questions fed to online search engines.

Speaking of search engines, the Internet is the single largest gathering of human knowledge, so it's no wonder that sites like Let Me Google That For You exist to use mockingly as a response to questions that could easily be answered by spending a few seconds typing it into Google.

But not all questions can be Googled, and those that can have to have someone be the first one to ask it before the search engine crawls over and records the answer. Also, remember that when asking someone - in person or on an online forum or message board - you should be considerate of the time and effort they'll have to put into providing the answer.

Failing all else, if you cannot find the answer to your question, another method is to propose the wrong answer and watch as people flock to correct you. Known as Cunningham's Law, it is the Internet equivalent of a French saying "prêcher le faux pour savoir le vrai" - "preach the falsehood to know the truth.""

While I'm linking XKCD comics, I'd also like to encourage anyone that does find their answer, if you first asked on an online message board, please return to provide your working solution!
